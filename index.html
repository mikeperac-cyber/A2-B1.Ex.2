Tell a Travel Mishap

Tell a coherent story using background and completed actions.

Grammar focusPast Simple vs. Past ContinuousA travel mishap

01

Useful vocabulary

Listen, read the example, and say each word aloud.

delay

a period of waiting because something is late

“The flight delay lasted two hours.”

luggage

bags used while traveling

“My luggage arrived the next day.”

notice

to become aware of something

“I noticed my passport was missing.”

announcement

a public spoken message

“We heard an announcement at the gate.”

miss

to be too late for something

“They missed their connection.”

fortunately

used to introduce a lucky result

“Fortunately, a staff member helped us.”

02

Grammar in a nutshell

Form

Background: was/were + -ing. Main event: Past Simple.

Use

Combine the tenses to set a scene and move a story forward.

Example

We were waiting at the gate when the airline announced a delay.

Watch out

Do not use Past Continuous for every action; completed events need Past Simple.

Warm-up

Connect the idea

Think of a journey that did not go exactly as planned. What was the first problem?

03

Today’s practice

Tell a 90-second story about a real or imagined travel mishap.

Speaking brief

Set the scene, describe what people were doing, explain the problem, and finish with the result.

  1. Plan the setting, problem, action, and result.
  2. Use when or while twice.
  3. Include three Past Simple and three Past Continuous forms.
  4. Retell the story with clear sequence markers.
I was traveling to…While we were…Suddenly…Fortunately…
